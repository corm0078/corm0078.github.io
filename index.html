Poster design: the Great American Novels

The Challenge

The challenge was to create a series of movie posters for great American novels that were brought to the big screen. The poster should be iconic while keeping a classic feel that would remind the viewer of the original time period of the novel.

The Solution

The solution was creating an art deco poster that would remind the viewer of the time period for the original piece of work and creating interest with this style that leads the eye through the finish piece.

Say Emotion

The Challenge

The challenge was creating a series of Emoji that would pop out to the user based on emotions. Finding the correct Emoji on today's mobile phones should be simple but with the lack of colours and important features makes the task hard. The new icons needed to create an instant emotion from the user.

The Solution

The solution was creating Icons that would be recognize based on colour profiles mostly, then the user could select a more specific feeling . The bring colours reflect moods like red for anger or blue for sadness appealing the users psychological aspect. The Eyes were also made to be an important part of the icons by making them bigger and adding eyebrows. These little changes made it easier for the viewer to spot their icons.

Young Jane

The Challenge

Young Jane needed a logo that would appeal to their target audience with a young and fun design that would remind of the vintage old days without being old itself. The logo needed to create a sense of recognition for the company.

The Solution

The solution was creating the image of any woman so that the Young Jane customer could see herself in those shoes and ultimately decide to go to the shop. The brand colours were vintage colours that would capture the eye while helping define the woman figure and bring it to the next level. The half circle was added in order to create a link between the woman and the text making the logo stronger. This logo will stand the test of time and help the target audience relate with the Young Jane identity.

Creative Process

The creative process is my favorite part it is not just about the idea but how you got to the idea. Being a graphic designer is much more then having ideas its conveying a message through the idea to another person. This all begins in at this stage of the process.

Design

Designing is the most rewarding part of the process, getting your ideas to come to life on a screen or on a piece of paper is great. The design part might be challenging at time but its definitely worth the sweat and tears. From print to web nothing is off limits for me, design is fun no mater what the medium may be.

Interaction

After working on something for so long there is nothing better then showing it to peer or the target market/user to see what they think how you could improve it in the future. After all a project is never finished and improvements are just a small part in becoming the best designer possible.
